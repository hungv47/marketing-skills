# AI Platform Optimization Guide

Detailed optimization strategies for each major AI search platform.

---

## Google AI Overviews

**Data source:** Traditional Google Search index + structured data + Knowledge Graph

**What gets cited:**
- Content that already ranks well in traditional search
- Pages with FAQ and How-To schema markup
- Content with strong E-E-A-T signals (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)
- Featured snippet content (often directly pulled into AI Overviews)

**Optimization priority:**
1. Implement structured data (FAQ, How-To, Article schema)
2. Optimize for featured snippets (direct answers at top of content)
3. Maintain strong traditional SEO fundamentals
4. Ensure Google Business Profile is complete and accurate
5. Build entity associations in Knowledge Graph (Wikipedia, Wikidata)

**Unique consideration:** Google AI Overviews is the largest AI search surface by volume. If you're only optimizing for one platform, start here.

---

## ChatGPT (with browsing)

**Data source:** Bing index + GPTBot crawl data + training data

**What gets cited:**
- Third-party reviews and mentions (heavily weighted)
- Original research and proprietary data
- Well-structured comparison content
- Content from high-authority domains

**Optimization priority:**
1. Allow GPTBot in robots.txt
2. Build third-party mentions (review sites, industry publications)
3. Publish original data that can't be found elsewhere
4. Ensure consistent entity information across the web
5. Create comparison content with specific, verifiable claims

**Unique consideration:** ChatGPT heavily weights third-party sources over first-party claims. Your G2 profile may matter more than your own website for ChatGPT citations.

---

## Perplexity

**Data source:** PerplexityBot (own crawler) + Bing + multiple search APIs

**What gets cited:**
- Content with inline source citations (Perplexity loves content that itself cites sources)
- Structured comparison tables
- Direct answer passages (40-60 words)
- Recent content (Perplexity's crawler is aggressive about freshness)

**Optimization priority:**
1. Allow PerplexityBot in robots.txt
2. Cite sources within your content (creates a citation chain)
3. Use comparison tables extensively
4. Write clear 40-60 word answer blocks under H2 headings
5. Keep content fresh — update dates and data regularly

**Unique consideration:** Perplexity always shows its sources. Getting cited by Perplexity creates a visible backlink-like effect that users can click through.

---

## Claude

**Data source:** Training data + ClaudeBot crawl

**What gets cited:**
- Expert-attributed content with clear credentials
- Original research and data
- Nuanced, balanced analysis (not marketing fluff)
- Content that acknowledges tradeoffs and limitations

**Optimization priority:**
1. Allow ClaudeBot in robots.txt
2. Publish expert-authored content with clear attribution
3. Create balanced analysis that acknowledges limitations (Pratfall Effect)
4. Provide original data and unique insights
5. Maintain factual accuracy — Claude's training emphasizes accuracy

**Unique consideration:** Claude tends to prefer balanced, honest content over promotional material. Content that acknowledges tradeoffs may actually perform better than pure sales copy.

---

## Gemini

**Data source:** Google Search index + Knowledge Graph + Google Business Profile

**What gets cited:**
- Content in Google's index with strong entity signals
- Google Business Profile data
- Knowledge Graph entities
- YouTube content (Google-owned property)

**Optimization priority:**
1. Optimize Google Business Profile completely
2. Build Knowledge Graph presence (structured data, Wikipedia)
3. Publish on YouTube (Gemini has preferential access to YouTube data)
4. Implement Organization and Product schema
5. Maintain consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across the web

**Unique consideration:** Gemini's integration with Google's ecosystem means Google Business Profile and YouTube optimization have outsized impact compared to other AI platforms.

## Cross-Platform Optimization Checklist

These actions improve visibility across all AI platforms:

- [ ] Publish comparison content with structured tables
- [ ] Write 40-60 word answer passages under clear H2 headings
- [ ] Cite external sources within your content
- [ ] Attribute content to named experts with credentials
- [ ] Publish original research with specific numbers
- [ ] Maintain active review site profiles (G2, Capterra)
- [ ] Allow all AI crawlers in robots.txt (unless you have a specific reason to block)
- [ ] Implement FAQ, How-To, and Article schema
- [ ] Keep content fresh with regular updates
- [ ] Build third-party mentions through PR, guest content, and community participation
