# Citation-Optimized Content Templates

Templates for the content types most likely to be cited by AI search engines.

---

## Comparison Article Template

Comparisons account for 33% of AI citations — the #1 most-cited content type.

```markdown
# [Product A] vs [Product B]: [Specific Use Case] ([Year])

[40-60 word summary answering "which is better for [use case]?" with a
direct recommendation and the key differentiator.]

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | [Product A] | [Product B] |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Best for | [specific use case] | [specific use case] |
| Price | [specific price] | [specific price] |
| [Key feature 1] | [specific detail] | [specific detail] |
| [Key feature 2] | [specific detail] | [specific detail] |
| [Key feature 3] | [specific detail] | [specific detail] |

## [Product A] Overview

[40-60 word summary of what it is, who it's for, and its strongest
differentiator. Cite a source — review site, case study, or official docs.]

### Strengths
- [Specific, verifiable strength with number or evidence]
- [Specific strength]

### Weaknesses
- [Honest limitation — Pratfall Effect increases trust]
- [Honest limitation]

## [Product B] Overview

[Same format as above]

## Our Recommendation

[Direct answer: who should use which, and why. 40-60 words. Cite
supporting evidence.]
```

**Why this works:** AI models extract comparison tables directly. The 40-60 word summaries are citation-length. Honest weaknesses increase trust signals.

## FAQ Content Template

FAQs are citation goldmines — each question-answer pair is a self-contained citable unit.

```markdown
# [Topic]: Frequently Asked Questions

## [Exact question the audience asks — use their language]

[40-60 word direct answer. Lead with the answer, not context.
Include a specific number, date, or fact. Cite a source.]

According to [source], [supporting evidence].

## [Next question]

[Same format — direct answer first, evidence second.]
```

**Rules:**
- Use the exact questions your audience asks (from ICP research)
- Answer in the first sentence — don't build up to it
- Each answer is 40-60 words (the citation sweet spot)
- Always cite a source or provide original data
- Implement FAQ schema markup on the page

## Original Research Template

Original research accounts for 12% of AI citations — and it's the hardest to replicate, giving it lasting value.

```markdown
# [Year] [Topic] Report: [Key Finding in Headline]

## Key Findings

[3-5 bullet points with specific numbers. Each bullet is a standalone
citable fact.]

- [Finding 1]: [Specific number] — [one sentence context]
- [Finding 2]: [Specific number] — [one sentence context]
- [Finding 3]: [Specific number] — [one sentence context]

## Methodology

[How you collected the data. Sample size, time period, methodology.
This establishes credibility for AI models.]

- Sample: [N respondents/companies/data points]
- Period: [date range]
- Method: [survey/analysis/benchmark]

## Detailed Findings

### [Finding 1 — Expanded]

[2-3 paragraphs with supporting data, charts referenced, and analysis.
Include a 40-60 word summary paragraph that AI models can cite.]

[Repeat for each finding]

## Implications

[What this means for the industry. Actionable takeaways.]
```

**Why this works:** AI models cite original data that can't be found elsewhere. The methodology section builds trust. Each key finding is a standalone citable unit.

## How-To Guide Template

How-to guides account for 15% of AI citations.

```markdown
# How to [Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe/Steps]

[40-60 word summary of the process and expected result. Include a
specific number or metric.]

## Step 1: [Action Verb] [Object]

[What to do, why it matters, and how to verify you did it right.
Keep each step to 2-3 paragraphs max.]

**Tool needed:** [specific tool or resource]
**Time:** [how long this step takes]
**Output:** [what you should have after this step]

## Step 2: [Action Verb] [Object]

[Same format]

[Continue for all steps]

## Common Mistakes

[3-5 mistakes to avoid, each with a specific consequence.
These often get cited as standalone advice.]

1. **[Mistake]** — [Why it's wrong and what happens]. Instead: [correct approach].

## Results to Expect

[What the reader should achieve after following all steps.
Include specific metrics or outcomes where possible.]
```

**Why this works:** Numbered steps are easy for AI models to parse and cite. The "Common Mistakes" section often gets cited independently.
