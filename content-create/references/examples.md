### Content Brief: "Organized Panic" LinkedIn Carousel

#### Distribution Target

- **Channel:** LinkedIn
- **Placement:** Feed post (document/carousel)
- **Format:** PDF carousel
- **Specs:** 1080x1350 (4:5), 8 slides

#### Content Specification

- **Purpose:** Differentiate—make them say "This is different because it addresses the root cause, not symptoms"
- **Content Type:** Thesis-driven—state claim, back with proof
- **Narrative:** "Ops managers think more tools = more control, but systematic chaos is a process problem not a tool problem, because adding features to broken workflows just organizes the chaos, so they should fix the system before switching tools."

#### Creative Content

**Hook (Slide 1):**
```
Most project management
is just organized panic.

Here's how to tell if that's you.
→
```

**Body (Slides 2-7):**

**Slide 2:**
```
You have 47 open tabs.

12 are "project tracking."

3 are the same project
in different tools.

→
```

**Slide 3:**
```
Your team calls it "staying organized."

Reality:

You're not managing projects.
You're managing tools that
manage fragments of projects.

→
```

**Slide 4:**
```
The symptoms:

• Daily standup is a status update marathon
• "Let me check" is your most-used phrase
• You're the human router between systems
• Friday feels like a hostage situation

Sound familiar?

→
```

**Slide 5:**
```
The root cause:

You adopted tools
before you defined process.

Now your process is:
"Whatever the tools let us do."

→
```

**Slide 6:**
```
The shift:

Stop asking: "Which tool has the best features?"

Start asking: "What's our actual workflow?"

Tools should adapt to your team.
Not the other way around.

→
```

**Slide 7:**
```
Before you switch tools again:

1. Map your current workflow (on paper)
2. Identify where work actually gets stuck
3. Fix the process, not just the visibility
4. Then find a tool that fits

→
```

**CTA (Slide 8):**
```
We built [Product] for teams
who are done with organized panic.

One workflow. One source of truth.
No more being the human router.

See how it works →
[link in comments]
```

**Caption:**
```
I've seen teams with 6 project management tools somehow have less clarity than a team with sticky notes.

The tools aren't the problem.

The organized panic is.

Here's the 7-slide breakdown on how to tell if your "project management" is actually just sophisticated chaos—and what to do about it.

Swipe through, then tell me: which symptom hit hardest?

#projectmanagement #operations #productivity
```

#### Production Guidelines

**Visual Direction:**
- Mood: Clean, confident, slightly provocative
- Color palette: Brand primary on slide 1 and 8, neutral backgrounds on 2-7
- Typography: Bold sans-serif headlines, generous white space
- Imagery style: Text-forward, minimal graphics—let copy carry

**Tone for this channel:**
- More professional than X
- Longer sentences OK
- Can use em dashes for emphasis
- No emojis in slide content (OK in caption)

**Do's:**
- Use line breaks for rhythm
- Keep each slide to one idea
- Make slide 1 scroll-stopping
- End with clear next step

**Don'ts:**
- Don't add stock photos
- Don't put CTA on every slide
- Don't use bullet points on every slide
- Don't make it feel like a pitch deck

**Assets Needed:**
- [ ] 8 slides at 1080x1350 (4:5)
- [ ] Brand colors and fonts
- [ ] Logo for slide 8
- [ ] Landing page URL for tracking

### Content Brief: "Natural Skincare" TikTok

#### Distribution Target

- **Channel:** TikTok
- **Placement:** In-feed video
- **Format:** Vertical video
- **Specs:** 9:16 (1080x1920), 45 seconds, burned-in captions

#### Content Specification

- **Purpose:** Validate and agitate—make them say "I knew something was off with these products"
- **Content Type:** Thesis-driven—state claim, back with proof
- **Narrative:** "Skincare buyers think 'natural' labels mean clean ingredients, but 'natural' is an unregulated marketing term, because brands can use it without meeting any standard, so they should learn the 3 things to actually check."

#### Creative Content

**Hook (0:00-0:03):**

Text on screen: `Your "natural" skincare probably isn't`

Voiceover: "Your natural skincare probably isn't natural."

Visual: Founder holding popular "natural" skincare product, skeptical expression

---

**Setup (0:03-0:10):**

Text on screen: `"Natural" isn't regulated`

Voiceover: "The word 'natural' isn't regulated by the FDA. Any brand can slap it on a label. It literally means nothing."

Visual: Quick cut to label showing "natural" claim, then cut back to founder

---

**Main Content (0:10-0:35):**

Text on screen: `What to actually check:`

Voiceover: "Here's what to actually look for."

Visual: Founder flipping product to show ingredients

---

Text on screen: `1. First 5 ingredients`

Voiceover: "One. The first five ingredients. That's 80% of your product. If you can't pronounce them, look them up."

Visual: Pointing at ingredient list

---

Text on screen: `2. "Fragrance" = red flag`

Voiceover: "Two. The word 'fragrance.' It's a catch-all that can hide hundreds of undisclosed chemicals. Actual clean brands list their scent sources."

Visual: Circling "fragrance" on label

---

Text on screen: `3. Certifications > claims`

Voiceover: "Three. Look for actual certifications—EWG Verified, COSMOS, USDA Organic. Not vibes. Certifications."

Visual: Showing certification logos

---

**CTA (0:35-0:45):**

Text on screen: `We list everything. Every. Single. Ingredient.`

Voiceover: "We don't hide behind 'natural.' We list everything. Every ingredient, every source, every reason it's there. Link in bio if you want skincare that's actually clean."

Visual: Showing brand product with full ingredient transparency, then end frame with logo

---

**Caption:**
```
Wait til you see what's hiding in "natural" skincare...

What's the sketchiest ingredient you've found? Drop it below

#skincare #cleanbeauty #naturalskincare #ingredients #skincarecheck
```

#### Production Guidelines

**Visual Direction:**
- Mood: Educational exposé, slightly investigative
- Color palette: Natural lighting, minimal brand colors until CTA
- Typography: Bold, readable captions (white text, black outline)
- Imagery style: Talking head, product close-ups, text overlays

**Tone for this channel:**
- Fast-paced, punchy sentences
- Conversational, like talking to a friend
- Slightly provocative/skeptical
- No corporate speak

**Do's:**
- Hook within 1 second
- Use pattern interrupt at 3 seconds (cut or motion)
- Keep captions in safe zone (avoid top 150px, bottom 270px)
- Make it watchable without sound

**Don'ts:**
- Don't start with "Hey guys"
- Don't use slow transitions
- Don't make it feel like an ad until CTA
- Don't use generic stock music

**Assets Needed:**
- [ ] Vertical video (1080x1920)
- [ ] Burned-in captions (white, black outline)
- [ ] "Natural" competitor product (face obscured)
- [ ] Brand product for CTA
- [ ] Trending or original audio
- [ ] End frame with logo + "Link in bio"

### Content Brief: "Status Update Tax" X Thread

#### Distribution Target

- **Channel:** X
- **Placement:** Thread
- **Format:** Text thread with images
- **Specs:** 10 tweets, 280 characters each, 2 chart images

#### Content Specification

- **Purpose:** Validate and agitate—make them say "This is exactly what's killing our velocity"
- **Content Type:** Evidence-driven—numbers first, context second
- **Narrative:** "Engineering managers think standups and status updates keep teams aligned, but excessive reporting rituals are the #1 hidden cost to engineering velocity, because context-switching and meeting prep compound into hours of lost deep work, so they should measure actual shipping speed instead."

#### Creative Content

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
```
We tracked how engineers at 200+ companies actually spend their time.

The biggest productivity killer wasn't tech debt or bad tooling.

It was status updates.

Here's what the data shows:

🧵
```

**Tweet 2:**
```
The average engineer spends 5.2 hours/week on "alignment activities":

• Daily standups
• Status reports
• Slack check-ins from managers
• Jira ticket grooming
• "Quick sync" meetings

That's 13% of their working time reporting on work instead of doing it.
```

---

**Tweet 3:**
```
Here's what that actually costs:

A 10-person engineering team at $150k avg salary:

5.2 hrs/week × 10 engineers × 52 weeks = 2,704 hours/year

That's $195,000/year in salary spent on telling people what you're doing.

Not doing it. Telling people about it.
```

**Tweet 4:**

```
But the real cost is worse.

Every status update interrupts a flow state.

A 15-minute standup doesn't cost 15 minutes.
It costs 38 minutes of productive time.
```

## Quick Reference: Content Type by Hook

| Hook Type | Content Structure | Example Opening |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Question | Question → Exploration → Answer | "Have you ever wondered why...?" |
| Bold claim | Claim → Evidence → Reframe | "Everything you know about X is wrong." |
| How-to | Promise → Steps → Result | "Here's how to X in Y steps." |
| Story | Setup → Conflict → Resolution | "Last week I made a mistake..." |
| Data | Stat → Context → Implication | "73% of teams fail at this." |
| Sneak peek | Tease → Reveal → Access | "Here's what most people don't see..." |
| Contrarian | Common belief → Counter → Proof | "Stop doing X. Do this instead." |
| Analogy | Complex thing = Simple thing | "Think of X like Y." |
