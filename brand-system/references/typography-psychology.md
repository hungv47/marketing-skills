# Typography Psychology

Font selection based on emotional resonance, not trends.

## Font Categories & Emotional Mapping

### Geometric Sans (Clean, Modern, Neutral)
**Fonts**: Futura, Avenir, Proxima Nova, Circular, Product Sans, SF Pro
**Feels like**: Tech-forward, clean, optimistic, accessible
**Best for**: Apps, SaaS, startups, modern brands
**Avoid when**: You need warmth, tradition, or gravitas

### Humanist Sans (Warm, Friendly, Approachable)
**Fonts**: Gill Sans, Frutiger, Open Sans, Lato, Source Sans
**Feels like**: Trustworthy, human, clear, democratic
**Best for**: Healthcare, education, government, B2C products
**Avoid when**: You need edginess or luxury

### Grotesque Sans (Industrial, Serious, No-nonsense)
**Fonts**: Helvetica, Arial, Univers, Akzidenz Grotesk
**Feels like**: Corporate, reliable, neutral to cold
**Best for**: Enterprise, institutions, when you don't want the font to speak
**Avoid when**: You need personality or warmth

### Neo-Grotesque (Contemporary, Refined)
**Fonts**: Inter, Aktiv Grotesk, Graphik, Neue Haas Grotesk
**Feels like**: Modern professional, subtle sophistication
**Best for**: Design-conscious products, portfolios, premium SaaS
**Caution**: Inter is overused. Consider alternatives.

### Old-Style Serif (Traditional, Established, Trustworthy)
**Fonts**: Garamond, Caslon, Palatino, Georgia
**Feels like**: Historical weight, credibility, scholarship
**Best for**: Publishing, law, finance, luxury with heritage
**Avoid when**: You need speed or modernity

### Transitional Serif (Balanced, Authoritative)
**Fonts**: Times New Roman, Baskerville, Bookman
**Feels like**: Authoritative, readable, formal
**Best for**: Long-form content, editorial, reports
**Avoid when**: Targeting younger audiences or casual contexts

### Modern Serif (Elegant, High-contrast, Fashion)
**Fonts**: Bodoni, Didot, Playfair Display
**Feels like**: Luxury, fashion, dramatic, refined
**Best for**: Fashion, luxury brands, editorial headlines
**Avoid when**: Body text or screen reading (too high contrast)

### Slab Serif (Bold, Confident, Sturdy)
**Fonts**: Rockwell, Clarendon, Roboto Slab, Zilla Slab
**Feels like**: Strong, dependable, slightly vintage, bold
**Best for**: Headlines, CTAs, brands wanting presence
**Avoid when**: Elegance or subtlety is needed

### Display/Decorative (Distinctive, Memorable)
**Fonts**: Varies widely
**Feels like**: Unique personality, specific mood
**Best for**: Headlines, logos, brand moments
**Rule**: Use sparingly. One display font max. Never for body.

### Monospace (Technical, Code, Data)
**Fonts**: JetBrains Mono, Fira Code, SF Mono, IBM Plex Mono
**Feels like**: Technical precision, developer-friendly, raw
**Best for**: Code, data tables, technical products
**Avoid when**: Warmth or approachability is needed

## Pairing Principles

1. **Contrast in style, harmony in spirit**: Serif headline + Sans body works. Two similar sans fonts compete.

2. **Match x-height**: Fonts with similar x-heights feel cohesive when paired.

3. **One personality, one workhorse**: Display font for personality, neutral font for content.

4. **Historical compatibility**: Fonts from similar eras often pair naturally.

## Audience-Specific Recommendations

| Audience | Recommended Categories | Avoid |
|----------|----------------------|-------|
| Gen Z | Geometric sans, expressive display | Corporate serif, Times |
| Millennials | Humanist sans, neo-grotesque | Overly traditional |
| Professionals | Transitional serif, clean sans | Playful display |
| Enterprise | Grotesque, transitional | Trendy, decorative |
| Luxury | Modern serif, refined sans | Generic, rounded |
| Health/Wellness | Humanist, warm sans | Cold, technical |
| Fintech | Geometric sans, clean mono | Frivolous display |
| Education | Humanist, readable serif | Condensed, decorative |

## Weight Usage

- **Regular (400)**: Body text, descriptions
- **Medium (500)**: Emphasis, subheads, labels
- **Semibold (600)**: Headlines, buttons, important UI
- **Bold (700)**: Hero text, strong emphasis
- **Black (800-900)**: Display use only, sparingly

## Red Flags

- Using Inter, Roboto, or system fonts by default without intention
- Display fonts in body text
- More than 2 font families
- Too many weights (stick to 2-3 per family)
- Font doesn't match product's emotional territory
