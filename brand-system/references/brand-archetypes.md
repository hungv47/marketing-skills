# Brand Archetypes

The 12 Jungian brand archetypes — universal patterns of character that anchor all identity decisions. Select one primary (70%) and one secondary (30%) archetype. Every visual and verbal choice flows from this selection.

## The 12 Archetypes

### The Innocent

**Core desire**: Happiness, simplicity, paradise
**Greatest fear**: Punishment, doing something wrong
**Strategy**: Do the right thing, be optimistic, keep it simple
**Gift to audience**: Faith, optimism, simplicity

**Brands**: Coca-Cola, Dove, Aveeno, Whole Foods, Innocent Drinks

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Whites, pastels, soft yellows, light blues, clean greens. High brightness, low saturation. Fresh and uncluttered.
- **Typography**: Simple, clean, rounded sans-serifs. Open letterforms. Nothing aggressive or high-contrast. (Circular, Nunito, Poppins)
- **Photography**: Natural light, bright and airy, genuine smiles, simple compositions, nature and open spaces. No shadows or drama.
- **Radius**: Soft to rounded (0.5rem+). Everything feels gentle.

**Voice**: Optimistic, honest, simple. Short sentences. Active voice. Positive framing ("you can" not "don't miss out"). Never cynical, sarcastic, or complex.

---

### The Everyman (Regular Guy/Gal)

**Core desire**: Belonging, connection
**Greatest fear**: Being left out, standing out negatively
**Strategy**: Develop solid virtues, be relatable, common sense
**Gift to audience**: Realism, empathy, lack of pretense

**Brands**: IKEA, Target, Gap, Budweiser, Levi's

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Warm neutrals, earth tones, approachable blues and reds. Nothing too bright or too dark. Comfortable mid-range saturation.
- **Typography**: Humanist sans-serifs. Warm, readable, unassuming. No display fonts or anything flashy. (Open Sans, Lato, Source Sans Pro)
- **Photography**: Real people in real situations. Diverse, relatable. Not aspirational — achievable. Group shots, community, everyday life.
- **Radius**: Moderate (0.375-0.5rem). Friendly but not childish.

**Voice**: Down-to-earth, inclusive, friendly. Uses "we" and "you." Plain language. Never elitist, exclusive, or overly polished. Avoids jargon.

---

### The Hero

**Core desire**: Mastery, proving worth through action
**Greatest fear**: Weakness, vulnerability, quitting
**Strategy**: Be as strong and competent as possible, rise to challenges
**Gift to audience**: Inspiration, motivation, courage

**Brands**: Nike, Under Armour, BMW, FedEx, Gatorade

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Bold reds, dark blues, black, metallic accents. High contrast. Punchy and dynamic. Strong primary color with minimal palette.
- **Typography**: Bold, heavy sans-serifs. Strong geometry. Uppercase works. Condensed faces for impact. (Bebas Neue, Montserrat ExtraBold, Anton, Oswald)
- **Photography**: Dynamic action, motion, determination. Strong subjects. Dramatic lighting, high contrast. Sweat, effort, triumph.
- **Radius**: Sharp to minimal (0-0.25rem). No softness. Edges mean business.

**Voice**: Direct, commanding, inspirational. Short punchy sentences. Imperative mood ("Just do it"). Never weak, hesitant, or wordy. Acknowledges struggle but always resolves with triumph.

---

### The Outlaw (Rebel)

**Core desire**: Revolution, liberation, breaking rules
**Greatest fear**: Conformity, powerlessness, being co-opted
**Strategy**: Disrupt, shock, destroy what isn't working
**Gift to audience**: Freedom from convention

**Brands**: Harley-Davidson, Virgin, Diesel, Jack Daniel's

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Black as dominant, deep reds, dark oranges, raw metallics. Distressed textures. Gritty, high-contrast.
- **Typography**: Edgy, unconventional. Rough textures, hand-drawn, condensed bold. Anti-corporate. (Custom faces, distressed types, deliberately imperfect)
- **Photography**: Raw, unpolished, documentary-style. Grain, high contrast, desaturated. Counter-culture subjects, rebellion, individuality.
- **Radius**: Sharp (0). Angular, never rounded. Rounded feels safe — the Outlaw doesn't do safe.

**Voice**: Provocative, bold, unapologetic. Challenges the status quo directly. Can use slang, fragments, profanity (if brand appropriate). Never corporate, meek, or apologetic.

---

### The Explorer

**Core desire**: Freedom, discovery, self-actualization
**Greatest fear**: Conformity, feeling trapped, inner emptiness
**Strategy**: Journey, seek new experiences, escape the mundane
**Gift to audience**: Autonomy, ambition, authenticity

**Brands**: Jeep, The North Face, Patagonia, National Geographic, Subaru

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Earthy greens, sky blues, burnt oranges, warm browns, sand. Natural palette inspired by landscapes. Warm undertones.
- **Typography**: Rugged, expansive typefaces. Slab serifs for grounding, clean sans for readability. Nothing precious or decorative. (Roboto Slab, Source Serif, Libre Franklin)
- **Photography**: Vast landscapes, natural light, adventure, outdoor subjects. Wide compositions, sense of scale. Authentic — not stock.
- **Radius**: Minimal to moderate (0.25-0.375rem). Practical, not decorative.

**Voice**: Adventurous, authentic, empowering. Second person ("you"). Encourages action and exploration. Uses sensory language. Never restrictive, corporate, or timid.

---

### The Creator

**Core desire**: Innovation, enduring vision, self-expression
**Greatest fear**: Mediocrity, stale thinking, unrealized vision
**Strategy**: Develop artistic control and technical skill, create value through originality
**Gift to audience**: Innovation, imagination, creative confidence

**Brands**: Apple, Lego, Adobe, GoPro, Moleskine

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Distinctive, often unexpected combinations. Can range from minimalist (Apple's white + one accent) to vibrant (Adobe's multi-color). Always intentional, never arbitrary.
- **Typography**: Distinctive, often custom typefaces. Clean but with personality. Confident letter spacing. Can break conventional hierarchy for artistic effect. (SF Pro, Neue Haas Grotesk, custom faces)
- **Photography**: Beautiful composition, strong art direction. Product as hero. Clean, considered, often minimal. Shows craft and attention to detail.
- **Radius**: Varies by sub-expression. Often clean and precise (Apple) or playfully geometric (Lego).

**Voice**: Visionary, passionate about craft, confident but not arrogant. Celebrates creativity and innovation. Uses "create," "imagine," "design," "build." Never follows trends or sounds like everyone else.

---

### The Ruler

**Core desire**: Control, order, prosperity, stability
**Greatest fear**: Chaos, being overthrown, vulnerability
**Strategy**: Exercise power, create order and standards, lead
**Gift to audience**: Status, security, stability

**Brands**: Mercedes-Benz, Rolex, Louis Vuitton, American Express, Bloomberg

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Black, deep navy, dark purple, gold, silver. Restrained palette. Dark and commanding. Minimal color, maximum sophistication.
- **Typography**: Regal serifs, thin-to-thick contrast. Elegant, orderly, restrained. Generous spacing. (Didot, Bodoni, Times New Roman, Playfair Display)
- **Photography**: Polished, high-production. Dark lighting, dramatic shadows. Luxurious textures, precision, craftsmanship. No clutter.
- **Radius**: Sharp to minimal (0-0.125rem). Precision and authority. Round feels casual — the Ruler is not casual.

**Voice**: Authoritative, confident, measured. Formal register. Short, declarative sentences. Uses "we" sparingly (the brand speaks, not a person). Never informal, uncertain, or populist.

---

### The Magician

**Core desire**: Transformation, making dreams reality
**Greatest fear**: Stagnation, unintended negative consequences
**Strategy**: Create unique experiences, deliver "wow" moments, transform
**Gift to audience**: Vision of what's possible

**Brands**: Disney, Dyson, Tesla, Polaroid, TED

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Deep purples, midnight blues, golds, silvers, black. Mystical and transformative. Gradients can work here (and almost nowhere else). Rich saturation.
- **Typography**: Distinctive, often with a touch of elegance. Can be serif or sans — but never ordinary. Slightly unusual choices that feel "special." (Freight Text, Canela, GT Sectra)
- **Photography**: Transformative moments, before/after, wonder, surprise. Cinematic quality. Dramatic lighting, unexpected perspectives. Captures imagination.
- **Radius**: Moderate to soft (0.375-0.75rem). Not harsh, not childish. Smooth transitions, like magic.

**Voice**: Inspiring, visionary, wonder-driven. Paints pictures of transformation. "Imagine," "transform," "unlock," "discover." Speaks to possibility. Never mundane, technical-first, or limiting.

---

### The Lover

**Core desire**: Intimacy, connection, beauty, sensory experience
**Greatest fear**: Rejection, isolation, feeling unwanted
**Strategy**: Become desirable, evoke passion and desire
**Gift to audience**: Beauty, intimacy, passion

**Brands**: Chanel, Victoria's Secret, Godiva, Häagen-Dazs, Alfa Romeo

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Reds, burgundy, deep pinks, golds, warm blacks, cream. Rich and sensual. Warm undertones dominate. Saturation is high but not garish.
- **Typography**: Elegant serifs, flowing scripts (sparingly), high-contrast faces. Thin weights for body, bold for display. Sensual letterforms with contrast. (Didot, Playfair Display, Cormorant)
- **Photography**: Intimate, warm-lit, sensual. Close-ups, shallow depth of field, skin textures, luxurious materials. Evokes touch and desire.
- **Radius**: Soft to rounded (0.5rem+). Everything feels touchable, inviting, approachable.

**Voice**: Sensual, evocative, emotionally rich. Uses sensory language (texture, warmth, taste, touch). Intimate tone — speaks to one person, not a crowd. Never clinical, cold, or mass-market.

---

### The Caregiver

**Core desire**: Protecting and caring for others
**Greatest fear**: Selfishness, ingratitude, helplessness
**Strategy**: Do things for others, anticipate needs, give generously
**Gift to audience**: Safety, compassion, support

**Brands**: Johnson & Johnson, UNICEF, Campbell's, Volvo, TOMS

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Soft blues, warm greens, gentle yellows, warm neutrals. Calming, nurturing. Low to moderate saturation. Warm undertones.
- **Typography**: Humanist sans-serifs, warm and readable. Rounded letterforms. Nothing aggressive or angular. (Lato, Nunito Sans, Source Sans)
- **Photography**: People being cared for, human connection, softness, warmth. Natural light, gentle tones. Children, families, communities. Authentic, not staged.
- **Radius**: Soft to rounded (0.5-0.75rem). Everything feels safe and approachable.

**Voice**: Warm, empathetic, reassuring. "We're here for you." Patient, never rushed. Acknowledges feelings. Uses "we" and "together." Never cold, distant, or transactional.

---

### The Jester

**Core desire**: Joy, living in the moment, lightness
**Greatest fear**: Boredom, being boring, irrelevance
**Strategy**: Be playful, make people laugh, enjoy the present
**Gift to audience**: Joy, fun, permission to play

**Brands**: M&M's, Old Spice, GEICO, Dollar Shave Club, Mailchimp

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Bright, saturated, multi-color palettes. Yellow, orange, bright red, electric blue. High energy. Can use clashing colors intentionally. Nothing subdued.
- **Typography**: Rounded, playful, sometimes hand-drawn or custom. Can break rules (rotation, mixed sizes). Big personality. (Fredoka, Baloo, Quicksand, custom display)
- **Photography**: Candid, humorous, unexpected. Bright and saturated. Exaggerated expressions, playful compositions. Studio-lit fun.
- **Radius**: Rounded to pill (0.75rem-full). Everything feels fun and approachable.

**Voice**: Witty, irreverent, entertaining. Uses humor, wordplay, pop culture. Short, punchy. Breaks the fourth wall. Never boring, corporate, or preachy. Self-aware.

---

### The Sage

**Core desire**: Truth, wisdom, understanding
**Greatest fear**: Ignorance, misinformation, being misled
**Strategy**: Seek knowledge, share truth, help others understand
**Gift to audience**: Wisdom, intelligence, clarity

**Brands**: Google, BBC, TED, Harvard, The Economist, Wikipedia

**Visual identity mapping**:
- **Colors**: Muted blues, grays, whites, deep greens, golds. Subdued, intellectual. Low saturation, high clarity. Clean and authoritative.
- **Typography**: Clean, authoritative typefaces. Serif for gravitas, sans for modernity. Excellent readability prioritized above all. Generous line height. (Georgia, Source Serif, Inter, IBM Plex)
- **Photography**: Clean, informational, well-lit. Documentary quality. Data visualization as imagery. Maps, diagrams, real-world subjects. Clarity over style.
- **Radius**: Minimal to moderate (0.25-0.375rem). Clean and structured. Not decorative.

**Voice**: Informed, clear, analytical. Explains complexity simply. Uses evidence and data. Authoritative but accessible. "Research shows," "Here's what we know." Never dumbed-down, sensationalist, or opinionated without evidence.

## Archetype Selection Guide

### Step 1: Identify Primary Archetype

Ask: What is the brand's **core desire** and **fundamental strategy** for serving its audience?

| If the brand exists to... | Consider... |
|--------------------------|-------------|
| Make people feel happy and safe | Innocent |
| Help people belong | Everyman |
| Empower people to overcome challenges | Hero |
| Challenge the status quo | Outlaw |
| Help people discover and be free | Explorer |
| Enable creative expression | Creator |
| Provide order, stability, leadership | Ruler |
| Transform people's reality | Magician |
| Create intimate, beautiful experiences | Lover |
| Protect and care for people | Caregiver |
| Bring joy and lightness | Jester |
| Share knowledge and truth | Sage |

### Step 2: Add Secondary Archetype (30%)

The secondary archetype adds differentiation. Choose one that:
- Doesn't contradict the primary (Hero + Jester is unusual; Hero + Sage works)
- Adds a dimension competitors in your category lack
- Reflects a genuine aspect of the brand, not wishful thinking

### Step 3: Validate

Test the archetype blend against:
- Does it match how the founding team actually talks and thinks?
- Would customers recognize this personality if described to them?
- Does it create space that competitors don't occupy?
- Can you sustain it across every touchpoint, even boring ones (invoices, error messages)?

## Archetype Pairs That Work Well

| Primary | Good Secondary | Why It Works |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| Hero | Sage | Competence backed by wisdom (Nike + knowledge) |
| Explorer | Creator | Discovery through invention (GoPro) |
| Sage | Magician | Wisdom that transforms (TED) |
| Caregiver | Innocent | Pure, protective warmth (Johnson & Johnson) |
| Ruler | Creator | Elegant innovation (Apple) |
| Outlaw | Jester | Rebellion with humor (Virgin) |
| Everyman | Caregiver | Relatable and caring (IKEA) |
| Magician | Creator | Transformative craft (Tesla, Dyson) |

## Archetype Conflicts to Avoid

| Combination | Why It Clashes |
|-------------|---------------|
| Ruler + Jester | Authority undermined by humor |
| Innocent + Outlaw | Purity contradicts rebellion |
| Caregiver + Outlaw | Nurturing contradicts disruption |
| Sage + Jester | Credibility undermined by jokes |
